Who am I?

I am resilient team player and a quick learner who is flexible and adaptable to new environments.
At first, I have been told, I may appear to be a somewhat reserved fellow, but that barrier can be easily broken with a cup of coffee and tech talk.

I believe in a just and an equal-opportunity society where an individual's financial capabilities, social background or political ideals are not a determinant as to what they can achieve or what opportunities are availed to them. I believe that we can achieve an equal society in which everyone is given an equal chance. It is for this reason that I have always trusted in the power of information technology to transform society. Information technologies continue to place the power to transform society in the hands of those who would otherwise be voiceless in society.
It is thanks to the information technology burst, that people from remote parts of the world can get real-time news from the rest of the world, that farmers in sub-saharan Africa can sell their produce to Europe, that news editors in North America can reach a global audience with the click of a button, that young people in East Asia can interract with their Australian counterparts in education exchange programs.
When you think about it, it's all so magical, yet so simple. Makes you wonder what's in the future.

I am interested greatly in information systems security, systems administration, database design, artificial intelligence and data mining.

I am an avid computer programmer with knowledge of C, C#, C++, bash scripting and PHP. I am currently learning Python with the aim of applying it in penetration testing.

It goes without saying that my favourite operating system is Ubuntu (this website was originally developed and tested on Kubuntu 17.10). I have worked with Ubuntu servers, Windows Server 2008 R2, Windows Server 2012 R2, Windows Server 2016, Windows 7, 8.1 and 10, Debian Jessie and Stretch, as well as Fedora.
